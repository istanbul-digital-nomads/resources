# Food and Groceries in Istanbul

## Eating Out

Istanbul is a food paradise. You can eat incredibly well at every budget level.

### Budget Meals
- **Lokanta/Esnaf lokantasi** (workers' restaurants): Home-style Turkish food, 80 to 150 TL for a full meal. Look for places where locals eat.
- **Doner and kebab shops:** 60 to 120 TL for a filling meal.
- **Simit:** The iconic Turkish bread ring, 10 to 20 TL. Great for a quick snack.
- **Pide and lahmacun:** Turkish pizza/flatbread, 80 to 150 TL.
- **Street food in Kadikoy:** The fish market area has amazing affordable food.

### Mid-Range
- **Casual restaurants in Kadikoy, Besiktas, Cihangir:** 200 to 500 TL per person.
- **Meyhane** (Turkish tavern): Great for a social dinner with meze and raki, 400 to 800 TL per person.

### Food Delivery Apps
- **Yemeksepeti** — The biggest food delivery app in Turkey
- **Getir Yemek** — Fast delivery, good restaurant selection
- **Trendyol Yemek** — Growing platform with good deals

## Groceries

### Supermarkets
- **Migros:** Mid-range, good quality, widespread
- **A101:** Budget-friendly, basics
- **BIM:** Budget-friendly, basics
- **Sok:** Budget-friendly, basics
- **CarrefourSA:** Larger stores with wider selection
- **Macro Center:** Premium, imported goods

### Markets
- **Weekly street markets (pazar):** Fresh fruits, vegetables, cheese, olives at great prices. Every neighborhood has one on a specific day. Ask locals for the schedule.
- **Kadikoy market:** Open daily, amazing fresh produce and specialty items.

### Grocery Delivery
- **Getir:** 10 to 15 minute delivery for basics and snacks
- **Migros Sanal Market:** Full grocery delivery
- **Trendyol Market:** Good selection and prices

## Tips

- Turkish breakfast is legendary. Try it at a cafe at least once (but it's cheaper to make at home).
- Tap water is safe in most of Istanbul but most people drink filtered or bottled water.
- Learn a few food words in Turkish. It makes ordering much easier and locals appreciate it.
- Vegetarian/vegan options are growing but traditional Turkish food is meat-heavy. Lokanta restaurants always have vegetable dishes though.
