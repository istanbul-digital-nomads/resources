# Healthcare in Istanbul

## Hospitals

Istanbul has excellent private hospitals with English-speaking staff. Quality is on par with Western Europe at a fraction of the cost.

### Top Private Hospitals
- **Acibadem** - Multiple locations, excellent reputation, most doctors speak English
- **Memorial** - Modern facilities, popular with expats
- **Liv Hospital** - Part of the Memorial group, high-tech facilities
- **American Hospital** - Long-established, strong international patient services
- **Florence Nightingale** - Good all-around care

### Public Hospitals
Free or very cheap for those with Turkish health insurance (SGK). Quality varies. Wait times can be long. English-speaking staff is less common.

## Health Insurance

**For short stays (under 90 days):** Your travel insurance should cover you. Make sure it's valid in Turkey.

**For residence permit applications:** You'll need health insurance valid in Turkey. Options:
- Turkish private health insurance (Allianz, Axa, Mapfre, Acibadem Sigorta) starting from 5,000 to 15,000 TL/year
- International plans that cover Turkey (SafetyWing, World Nomads, Cigna Global)

**SGK (Turkish social security):** If you have a residence permit, you can apply for SGK after one year of residence. Monthly cost depends on your situation.

## Pharmacies (Eczane)

Pharmacies are everywhere and pharmacists are knowledgeable. Many medications that require prescriptions in other countries are available over the counter in Turkey. Prices are very affordable.

Look for the green "ECZANE" signs. There's always a "nobetci eczane" (duty pharmacy) open 24/7 in each district.

## Dental and Eye Care

Both are affordable and high-quality in Istanbul. Many nomads specifically come to Turkey for dental work. A dental cleaning costs around 500 to 1,000 TL. Laser eye surgery starts around 15,000 to 25,000 TL.

## Tips

- Save the number 112 (emergency services) in your phone.
- Private hospitals usually require upfront payment, then you claim from insurance.
- Keep digital copies of your insurance documents on your phone.
- For non-emergencies, ask in the Telegram group for doctor recommendations.
