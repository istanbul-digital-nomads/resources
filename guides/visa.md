# Visa and Legal Guide for Istanbul

## Tourist Visa

Most nationalities can enter Turkey visa-free or with an e-visa for 30 to 90 days. Check your specific situation at evisa.gov.tr.

Common durations:
- **EU citizens:** 90 days in any 180-day period (visa-free)
- **US citizens:** 90 days with e-visa
- **UK citizens:** 90 days with e-visa
- **Canadian citizens:** 90 days with e-visa

The e-visa costs around $50 USD and takes minutes to get online.

## Residence Permit (Ikamet)

If you plan to stay longer than your visa allows, you'll need a residence permit.

**Basic requirements:**
1. Health insurance valid in Turkey
2. Proof of address (notarized rental agreement)
3. Proof of financial means (bank statement)
4. Biometric photos
5. Application through e-ikamet.goc.gov.tr

**Process:**
1. Apply online at e-ikamet.goc.gov.tr
2. Get an appointment at your local immigration office
3. Attend the appointment with all documents
4. Wait for processing (usually 2 to 8 weeks)
5. Pick up your ikamet card or have it mailed

**Tips:**
- Apply before your tourist visa expires
- Get your rental contract notarized (noter) before your appointment
- Bring extra copies of everything
- The process changes frequently, so check recent experiences in the Telegram group

## Digital Nomad Visa

Turkey introduced a digital nomad visa program. Requirements include:
- Proof of remote employment or freelance work
- Minimum monthly income (check current threshold)
- Health insurance
- Clean criminal record

This visa allows you to stay for up to one year with possible renewal. Details and requirements may change, so verify at the official Turkish immigration website.

## Tax Considerations

- Turkey generally taxes residents (183+ days per year) on worldwide income
- If you're on a short stay, your home country's tax rules likely still apply
- Consult a tax professional for your specific situation
- Some nomads use Turkish-registered companies for local invoicing
