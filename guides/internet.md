# Internet and SIM Cards in Istanbul

## Mobile Data

### Getting a SIM Card

The three main carriers are Turkcell, Vodafone, and Turk Telekom. You can buy a prepaid SIM at any of their shops (bring your passport).

**Important:** Turkey requires foreign phones to be registered within 120 days. After that, unregistered phones are blocked from using Turkish SIMs. Registration costs around 20,000 TL (2026 prices) and can be done at a tax office or through the carrier.

For stays under 120 days, just buy a prepaid SIM and use it without registration.

### Recommended Plans

- **Turkcell:** Best coverage overall. Tourist SIM packages start around 500 TL for 20GB.
- **Vodafone:** Good coverage and competitive prices. Similar tourist packages available.
- **Turk Telekom:** Cheapest option, slightly less coverage in some areas.

All three offer 4G/LTE with good speeds in urban areas (30 to 100+ Mbps).

## Home Internet

If you're renting a furnished apartment, internet may already be set up. If not:

- **Turk Telekom fiber:** Most common. Plans from 300 to 600 TL/month for 50 to 100+ Mbps.
- **Superonline (Turkcell):** Good fiber coverage. Similar pricing.
- **Installation:** Can take 1 to 2 weeks. Ask your landlord to help with the process.

## Backup Options

- **Mobile hotspot:** Your phone's data plan is a reliable backup.
- **Portable wifi devices (MiFi):** Available from carriers. Good if you need dedicated internet.
- **Cafe wifi:** Most cafes have decent wifi (20 to 50+ Mbps). See our cafe guide.
- **Coworking spaces:** Typically have the fastest and most reliable internet. See our coworking guide.

## Tips

- Always have a backup internet option for important calls and deadlines.
- Test speeds at speedtest.net when checking out a new cafe or apartment.
- VPNs work fine in Turkey and are recommended for privacy.
- Some websites and services may be blocked in Turkey. A VPN solves this.
