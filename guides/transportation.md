# Getting Around Istanbul

## Istanbulkart

Your most important card in Istanbul. It's a rechargeable transit card that works on all public transport: metro, bus, ferry, tram, and funicular.

**Where to get one:** Any metro station, major bus stops, or some kiosks. Costs about 100 TL for the card itself.

**How to top up:** Machines at metro stations, kiosks, or through the Istanbulkart app.

**Cost per ride:** Discounted fares when you use the card (much cheaper than buying tokens). Transfers within a time window are further discounted.

## Metro

Istanbul's metro network is expanding rapidly. Key lines:
- **M1:** Connects the airport area to the old city
- **M2:** Taksim to Haciosman (runs through Sisli, Levent, Maslak)
- **M4:** Kadikoy to Sabiha Gokcen Airport area
- **Marmaray:** The line that crosses under the Bosphorus connecting Asian and European sides

## Ferries

The best way to cross the Bosphorus. Beautiful, affordable, and often faster than driving. Main routes:
- Kadikoy to Eminonu/Besiktas
- Uskudar to Eminonu/Besiktas/Kabatas

All ferries accept Istanbulkart. Check schedules on the Sehir Hatlari app.

## Buses

Extensive network covering the entire city. Use Google Maps for route planning. Can be very crowded during rush hours.

## Taxis and Rideshare

- **Taxis:** Yellow taxis are everywhere. Make sure the meter is running. Some drivers may try to take longer routes with tourists.
- **BiTaksi app:** Like Uber for taxis. Shows estimated price upfront. Recommended over hailing random taxis.
- **Uber:** Works in Istanbul but uses licensed taxi drivers.

## Tips

- Avoid driving in Istanbul unless you absolutely have to. Traffic is intense.
- The ferry commute between Kadikoy and Besiktas/Eminonu is genuinely enjoyable.
- Google Maps is reliable for public transit directions.
- Rush hours (7:30 to 9:30 AM and 5:00 to 7:30 PM) are brutal. Plan around them.
- Walking is great in most central neighborhoods but Istanbul is very hilly.
